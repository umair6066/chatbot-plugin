Custom suggestion chips

Suggestions are clickable quick-reply chips shown below the bot's messages. They guide users when they don't know what to ask, and auto-update based on context.

Open the chat and click any chip — it fires as a user message immediately. Then notice: after the bot replies, new context-aware chips appear. Chips on older messages grey out.

How suggestions work

Welcome message chips always shown

What products do you have? What's in stock? Show me prices Help me find something

Auto-generated from your product catalog. Pass a suggestions array to use your own.

After a product listing reply

Tell me about Wireless Headphones Tell me about Mechanical Keyboard Tell me about Desk Lamp

Context-aware — chips are generated from what was just listed.

When query is unclear — fallback chips

What products do you have? What's in stock? Show me prices Tell me about Wireless Headphones

When the bot says "I'm not sure I understood…" these chips rescue the conversation.

1 — Auto-generated (default)

<script src="./chatbot-widget.iife.js"></script>
<script>
  ChatbotWidget.init({
    products: [ /* your catalog */ ],
    // No suggestions key — auto-built from products
  });
</script>

2 — Custom welcome chips

<script src="./chatbot-widget.iife.js"></script>
<script>
  ChatbotWidget.init({
    suggestions: [
      "What products do you have?",
      "What's your return policy?",
      "Do you ship internationally?",
      "Talk to a human",
    ],
  });
</script>

3 — Business-specific chips (no products)

<script src="./chatbot-widget.iife.js"></script>
<script>
  ChatbotWidget.init({
    title:   "Support",
    suggestions: [
      "I have a billing question",
      "My order hasn't arrived",
      "I want to return something",
      "Talk to a human",
    ],
  });
</script>

Up to 4 chips fit comfortably on mobile. You can pass more — the row wraps — but 3–4 is the sweet spot.
